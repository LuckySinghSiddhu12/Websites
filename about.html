Breaking the Technology Code on Vertical Axle Wind Turbines

The Journey

In 2014 while researching how to make lagre quantities of Hydrogen to use a farm fuel.
It became obvious that a source of low cost electricity would be needed as grid electricity cost more than the value of the Hydrogen it produced.

In 1976 Mr. Spencer made a balsa wood model of a Vertical Windmill.
Drawing on that model he set forth researching its merits as a source for Hydrogen generation.He built tow research models 80ft(24 m) wide and 15ft (4.5 m) tall . The models were paramount in breaking the code for Vertical Axis Wind Turbines.

After 8 years, 30,000 hours, little sleep and a boat load of personal dollars, Mr Spencer was able to prove its commnerical wind farm merits.

Relative to today's current wind turbins's land space; the 3DWT is capable of producing 50X the amount of electricalm power.Equaling a massive leap in wind farm capacity, technology, and production.

One Patent has been issued and a second Patent has received a "Notice of Allowance" and will be issued.
